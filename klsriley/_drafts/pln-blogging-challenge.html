I was tagged in a PLN (Professional Learning Network) blogging challenge way back in December by my Twitter pal and fellow music educator Kevin Dengel (@kevindengel). I told him I'm barely able to keep up with the normal blogging demands of posting, editing video and photos, and the like... So nearly 8 months later, here it is!

The assignment:

  1. Acknowledge the nominating blogger.
  2. Share 11 random facts about yourself.
  3. Answer 11 questions the nominating blogger has created for you.
  4. List 11 bloggers.
  5. Post 11 questions for the bloggers you nominate and let the bloggers know they have been nominated. Don’t nominate a blogger who has nominated you.
  6. Post back here (in the comment section) with a link to your finished assignment.

 

My 11 Random Facts:

  1. My husband got the initial inspiration for our daughter's name, Cordelia from the name of a pioneer of electronic music, Delia Derbyshire.
  2. By my fifth year of teaching, I had taught in six schools in three districts.
  3. I met my husband in 2000, traded phone numbers with him in 2002, got engaged in 2006, and was married in 2007, all within a 100-yard radius.
  4. Because I started college at age 17, I could not get a public library card without a parent's permission, even though I lived on campus.
  5. I wish I could live in a Frank Lloyd Wright house, complete with period furniture, decor, and wardrobe.
  6. Sometimes I tell people I became a teacher because I love office supplies and bossing people around.
  7. I am a huge fan of board games and card games. A couple of my current favorites are 7 Wonders and Pillars of the Earth.
  8. I learned to play euchre from 7-year-olds at the YMCA day camp where I worked during my summers off from college. And I'm pretty good.
  9. My sister and I got stranded in Laramie, Wyoming with a broken down car when we were moving her from Columbus, Ohio to San Mateo, California in 2007. There is NOTHING to do in Laramie, Wyoming without a car.
  10. I am a wealth of useless knowledge, probably not quite good enough for Jeopardy, but I would clean up on Cash Cab.
  11. My dream "job" would be perpetual graduate student (I currently have a B.M., M.A., and four areas of teaching licensure). I think my next area of study might be architecture.

 

Answers to Kevin's 11 Questions:

  1. What (or who) made you decide to be an educator? I was good at music, and I enjoyed working with kids, so it just kind of happened. I explored quite a few career paths before making a decision, and it ultimately came down to the scholarship money I was offered to attend Conservatory.
  2. Who is the most intelligent person you know? My husband.
  3. Have you watched the entirety of the music video "What does the Fox Say?" Um, only a bazillion times. I introduced Cordelia's daycare teachers to it, and they're very grateful.
  4. If you were a giant mega monster what city would you rampage? San Francisco.
  5. What was your first mobile phone? I have no idea, but I've had the same mobile phone number since 2000!
  6. What potential talents do you think you might have if you worked at them? I think I could have been a dancer if I had gotten an early start. I enjoy writing and would love to write a book someday.
  7. What do you consider is the most important appliance in a house? At this point in my life, I will say the washing machine, because we use cloth diapers and babies make a lot of messes - and not just in the diapers. In a couple of years, I might have a different answer, though.
  8. What song sums you up? WOW. Hard question. I'm going to go with "Sweet Besty From Pike" because A) I identify with Betsy's determination, B) it's a folk song, which is kind of an important thing in my line of work, C) I'm a pushover for songs in triple meter, and D) I have loved that song since I was little.
  9. Name your favorite Great Lake. Come on, everyone's got one... Lake Erie, mostly because I love Cedar Point so much.
  10. Are you a morning person? Meh.
  11. What would you call your autobiography? Practically Perfect in Every Way. (That's supposed to be sarcastic. A little.)
