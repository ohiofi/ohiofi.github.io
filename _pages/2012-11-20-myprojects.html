In addition to the blog, OhioFi.com contains dozens of web apps that I have created. First and foremost are a series of apps for computer-assisted instruction that include Signal Flow, Microphones, Resistor Color Bands, and Ear Training. These apps are based in JavaScript and use PHP and MySQL in order to save student progress and maintain a scoreboard. I have seen a correlation between student use of these apps and test grades.

Phantom of Power is a Flash game that I created so that my MUS-110 Intro to Music Software students could create 8-bit music for it.

The MUS-110 Sampling Project allows students to upload samples to OhioFi. Samples can be triggered one at a time or a randomized song can be created by pressing the play button.

For the Interactive Multimedia Project, MUS-110 students create either a “Choose Your Own Adventure”-style interactive story, an interactive musical composition, or a multimedia quiz.

 

Tank War was a grad school project that involved creating a soundtrack and sound effects for a simple Flash game. To try to create a vintage lo-fi sound, I used YMCK's Magical 8-bit Plug and ran a few voice samples through a bitcrusher plugin.

 

 


Multitrack Buttons was my first attempt at creating an interactive Flash app from scratch. It was modelled after a game on the Boohbah website (a tv show for pre-schoolers).

 

 


Multitrack Faders was a reimplementation of the previous multitrack app. This time allowing the user to set individual volume levels rather than simply turning tracks on and off.

 

I have two other simple Flash projects shown here. The first is a Flash audio player with play, pause, and stop controls. The second is a looped Flash animation.
